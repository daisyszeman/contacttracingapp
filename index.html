Contact Tracing Applications

for combating COVID-19

What is contact tracing?

mid a global pandemic, our digital world naturally has turned to digital solutions
or contact tracing. Governments worldwide have seized this approach,
releasing apps to perform automated contact tracing. 
   

Contact tracing is the process of identifying and informing people that they have been exposed to an individual who tested positive for a virus so that they may take appropriate actions, such as isolating and testing. Traditionally, this was done manually through an interview with a patient about whom they were near during their infectious period. Then, the individuals at risk could be contacted. There could also be a public announcement of a location where a patient was and the time they were there. Digital contact tracing aims to perform an automated version of these interviews and notifications by collecting information from users and comparing this information to determine someone’s risk level. While a variety of protocols is adopted in different countries to perform contact tracing, resulting in widely differing security and privacy assurances. 

  




This analysis aims at picturing the usage of contact tracing applications worldwide, the techologies and approaches, as well as the the privacy concern.

How does contact tracing work?

  

    
A split has emerged between two different types of app - the so-called centralised and decentralised approaches.


Under the centralised model, data gathered from the app is uploaded to a centralised server where matches are made with other contacts, should a person start to develop Covid-19 symptoms.


By contrast, the decentralised model gives users more control over their information by keeping it on the phone. It is there that matches are made with people who may have contracted the virus.
    

  


  
  







  

    

    

      The common technologies used for contact tracing applications are as below:  
    

    
  
    
    
  • 
      Bluetooth-Based
      
    An app broadcasts an anonymous identifier over Bluetooth, while at the same time scanning for other devices broadcasting from the app. When two devices detect each other, they exchange identifiers and log them along with other auxiliary information, such as signal strength.
      
    
    
    • 
    
  • 
      GPS-Based
      
    The app logs the GPS location data of the phone. The data are either streamed to a server or stored locally. When someone tests positive, places they had been during the infectious period are identified. Other people that were there at the same time are found, using the location data on the server, or updating a publicly available map.
      
    
    
    • 
    
  • 
      Data mining (Non-app-based system)
      
    The system gathered tracking information from a variety of sources including mobile device tracking data and card transaction data, and combined these to generate notices via text messages to potentially-infected individuals.

Will contact tracing be a new form of digital surveillance?

  

    Contact tracing apps are the tech tool of the moment, yet without proper scrutiny, these tools have the potential to fundamentally alter the future of privacy and other human rights. These apps use a variety of protocols to perform contact tracing, resulting in widely differing security and privacy assurances.  
  



  

    In the article “A Security and Privacy Scoring System for Contact Tracing Apps”(Krehling, Essex 2021), an analysis of the privacy of contact tracing apps was performed with the intent of creating a metric of privacy. The applications were scrutinized through the lens of ten different privacy principles agreed by privacy and security experts,  scoring each application with a privacy score.
  




  

  
  
  


  













    
To further analyse the privacy of applications, all 46 apps are analysed on εxodus , which is a Privacy Auditing Platform for Android Applications. The trackers and permissions of each application are recorded. (*Applications on IOS platform is not analysed here) 
  

  
 Definition of terms:
  

  

  
Tracking

  
A tracker is a piece of software whose task is to gather information on the person using the application, on how they use it, or on the smartphone being used. 
  

  

  
Permissions

  
Permissions are actions the application can do on your phone.  Some of these permissions are tagged as dangerous. This means that from Google’s point of view, the application can do bad things using this permission. εxodus uses the Google permission classification.
  

  

  
Dangerous Permissions

  
Dangerous permission indicates a 'Dangerous' or 'Special' level according to Google's protection levels
.

What will be the next?

  

    

    
Contact tracing can be an important component of effective pandemic response, and contact tracing apps have the potential to support this objective. However, it is important to balance between contact tracing and privacy, to prevent it becomes a new form of digital surveiliance tool. 

 
The global coronavirus pandemic is entering its third year, after the short, sharp shock of Omicron in early 2022, the pandemic phase of COVID-19 looks to be ending for most locations. Some countries have already official ended the usage of contract tracing apps. However, it is sad that global pandenics are happening more frequently in the last few decades, how contact tracing should be done will be a continuing topic to discuss.
